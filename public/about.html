Cinema has always been a technology.

The movie camera was an engineering marvel before it was an art form. Every leap since — sound, color, digital editing, CGI, and now machine learning — arrived as a new machine that storytellers had to learn to speak through. Yonker Films is about that seam: where film and computing meet, and where they're heading next.

What this is

A working notebook on the future of technology and film. I write about the storied, often uneasy relationship between the two — the tools that reshaped how movies get made, the craft decisions hiding inside the software, and what it means to tell a human story through an increasingly computational medium.

Who's writing

I'm a filmmaker, editor, and storyteller — and I'm also learning professional software engineering. That double vantage is the whole point. I know what an editor feels when a cut lands, and I'm learning what the program underneath is actually doing. Most writing on this subject comes from one side of that line. I'm trying to write from the seam itself.

What you'll find here

  • Film & tech history — how computing quietly rewrote the craft of cinema.
  • Tools & craft — what's really happening inside the software editors and filmmakers use every day.
  • The road ahead — AI, virtual production, and where the medium goes from here.
  • Notes from learning to build — a filmmaker's view of becoming an engineer.
