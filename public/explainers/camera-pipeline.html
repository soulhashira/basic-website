How a Camera Sees

From photons hitting glass to pixels on a timeline — the complete data journey of a single frame.

Click any stage in the diagrams below to explore.

1 — Inside the Camera 2 — Card to Computer 3 — Inside the Edit 4 — Where AI Enters

1. Inside the Camera

A modern cinema camera is a computer with a lens mount. Light enters one end; a compressed data file exits the other. Between those two points, the signal transforms from analog photons into digital video through seven distinct stages.

2. Card to Computer

The card leaves the camera and enters a chain of custody. Every byte must survive the transfer intact — this is the one point in the pipeline where data loss is permanent and unrecoverable.

3. Inside the Edit

When you import footage into an NLE, the software reverses much of what the camera did — unpacking containers, decoding compressed frames, and reconstructing images in GPU memory for real-time playback.

4. Where AI Enters the Picture

Every stage above — from sensor debayering to codec compression to timeline rendering — is a computational process. Each one is a candidate for AI acceleration. Here is where the future is heading.
